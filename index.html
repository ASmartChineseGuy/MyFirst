FOUR LAWS TO GUIDE US

LAW1: Make it obvious

  Habit Scorecard You must be aware of your habits before you can change them.You can write down all your daily behaviors on a habits scorecard.Until you make the unconscious conscious, it will direct your life and you will call it fate. Using the formula."I will [behavior] at [time] in [the location]."

  Stacking Habits tie the desired habits with an existing habits. Using "After [current habit], i will [new habit]."

  Environment VS Motivation We are more influenced by our environment than our willpower or motivation. Environment is the invisible hand that shapes human behavior.

  Breaking a bad habit: Make it invisible.

LAW2: Make it attractive

  DopamineThe craving is what causes us to take action in the first place.

  How to use tempetation bundling Premack`s principle: "More probable behaviors reinforce less probable behaviors

  Group influence Find groups to join where the habit you desire is the norm.

  Where cravings come from Huma`s underlying motives:obtaining food and water, conserving energy, finding love and reproducing, connecting and bonding with others, winning social acceptance and approval, reducing uncertainty , achieving status and prestige. Online platforms and products do not invent new motivations, but rather appeal to the underlying motives of human nature. Your habits are modern-day solutions to ancient desires,new versions of old vices. The underlying motives behind human nature remain the same.

  How to reprogram your brain to enjoy hard habits You can make hard habits more attractive if you can learn to associate them with a positive experience. By highlighting the benefits of a habit rather than its downsides,you can quickly reprogram your mind and make it seem more appealing.

  Breaking a bad habit: Make it unattractive To break a bad habit, do the same but highlight the benefits of NOT doing that habit.

LAW3: Make it easy

  How long does it actually take to form a new habit Long before the neuroscientists dug into the process of forming habits,repetition was known as a powerful tool for establishing habits. You activate particular neuro circuits associated with habits every time you repeat them. So framing habit formation in terms of time is flawed. It should be framed in terms of the number of repetition.

  Reducing friction: the law of least effort By making your good habits more convenient, you`re more likely to stick with them. In order to build better habits, we have to find ways to decrease friction associated with good habits and increase friction associated with bad habits.

  Prime the environment for use By automating or setting up your environment, you can reduce the friction for future action.

  The 2-minute rule(stop procrastinating) Using the "2-minute rule" can help you establish small habits that will lead to success in bigger ones. Find a simple 2-minute version of your desired habit. You want to scale down your desired outcome. You need to get the routine anchored in place and then slowly build up the difficulty. After you have mastered the 2-minute habit, you can progress to the next phase.

  Breaking a bad habit: make it difficult Think about ways you can put barriers of friction between youself and the bad habit. Make it as impractical as possible.

LAW4: Make it satisfying

  The most important rule A feeling of pleasure is a message to the brain:"This feels good. Let`s repeat this next time." When you experiences pleasure, your brain learns that a behavior is worth remembering and repeating. "What is immediately rewarded is repeated, what is immediately punished is avoided." The first three laws increases your chances of doing the habit this time. The last law increases your chances of repeating the habit next time.

  The mismatch between immediate and delayed returns Ignoring an immediate reward for a long-term one. It is best to add a little immediate pleasure to the habits that will pay off in the long run and a little pain to those that won`t.

  Rewarding yourself and feeling successful The vital thing in getting a habit to stick is to feel successful——even if it`s in small ways. The feeling of success is the signal that your habit paid off and that the work was worth the effort. It is satisfying to make progress, and you can monitor your progress using visual measures. These "little wins" can go a long ways. Keeping a habit tracker may be the best method to monitor your progress.

  Habit tracking Using a habit tracker is simple way to determine whether you did a particular habit. Tracking becomes a reward.

  When life gets in the way (bad days) In spite of your best efforts, it is inevitable that life will interrupt you at some point. When you`re having a bad day, you don`t realize how valuable it is to just show up. "Lost days hurt you more than successful days help you." Don`t break the chain of continuity. Missing twice is the start of a bad habit; never do it.
